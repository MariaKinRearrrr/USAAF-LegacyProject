The Legends

Reflections and personal accounts from the veterans of the U.S. Army Air Forces.

First Lieut. Peter J. Kolas

Recalling his service as a pursuit pilot on a remote island, and the necessity of camouflage to protect the P-40 aircraft:

"I remember one they put up to assemble some P-40s under on a little island out there. I was a pursuit pilot then. The net worked fine. We weren't bothered by Jap bombers at all."

First Lieut. Peter J. Kolas

On the persistent threat and tactics of the Imperial Japanese forces:

"The Nips will bomb anything that looks like a plane."

First Lieut. Peter J. Kolas

Describing the extreme conditions at Guadalcanal, where maintenance was performed under pressure and with limited resources:

"The mechs would work on them while their ship was on a mission. About the only real lumber we had was from packing boxes flown in. But, there's one thing. On our Guadalcanal, the crew chief stands I saw were the regular metal kind."

First Lieut. Peter J. Kolas

A tribute to his crew chief, Miko, and the ingenuity that kept the aircraft operational against all odds:

"I guess I found out today how Miko really managed. How all the good boys managed to keep us flying, come hell or high water. Miko had it. It's the Daniel Boone in them."

War Department Citation

Posthumous recognition honoring the extraordinary heroism of Major John L. Jerstad during the low-level bombing mission against the Ploesti oil refineries in Romania:

"Major Jerstad set an example of heroism which will be an inspiration to the Armed Forces of the United States."

These accounts honor the legacy of the Guadalcanal veterans whose resilience ensured air superiority in the most challenging of theaters.
