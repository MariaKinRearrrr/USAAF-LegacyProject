In Memoriam

Final letters, last messages, and testaments of courage from those who gave their all.

Second Lieutenant John Gillespie Magee Jr.

Pilot and Poet; author of the immortal poem "High Flight," written shortly before he was killed in the line of duty:

"Oh! I have slipped the surly bonds of Earth and danced the skies on laughter-silvered wings... Put out my hand, and touched the face of God."

First Lieutenant Raymond R. "Ray" Sanders

Excerpt from his final letter sent home to his family before his aircraft disappeared:

"If anything should happen to me, don’t weep for me. Just remember that I died doing what I wanted to do most, and for a cause I believed was right."

Captain Guelich (Archival Record)

A recorded account of the miraculous survival and extraordinary skill demonstrated during the crash-landing of a C-54 transport in the Guiana jungle, saving all 22 lives aboard:

"This crash was skillfully handled and none of the 22 persons aboard was injured. The landing was made on but one engine, the other three cutting out within fifteen minutes before the crash."

"They shall mount up with wings as eagles; they shall run, and not be weary."
