The Unsung Heroes

Archival records of the ground crew and mechanical personnel of the USAAF.

Instructor

Regarding tactical awareness and emergency preparedness:

"We tell them, 'Suppose they bomb your tent?'"

Master Sgt. S. P. Bartczak (Senior Instructor)

Reflecting on the significance of aircraft maintenance and battle damage repair:

"We try to get the main ones. It all stands for something. Patching holes, that's from bullets. After every raid—and our siren is good and loud—the flight and crew chiefs go around slipping troubles in the ships that bombs might have caused."

Master Sgt. S. P. Bartczak (Senior Instructor)

On the philosophy of rigorous training:

"But, here, we are training them to expect the worst."

Student Mechanic

Encouraging a peer while manually cranking a P-40 aircraft engine:

"Make muscles, Skinny."

Instructor / Master Sgt. S. P. Bartczak

On the necessity of field ingenuity and improvisation:

"A mechanic is lost unless he has a way of hoisting things."

These records serve as a testament to the dedication and resilience of the mechanical personnel who supported the U.S. Army Air Forces.
